# StoneRise Demo Script — SOL Services
**Date:** 26 March 2026  
**Audience:** SOL Services (QSW & FM division)  
**Purpose:** Tailored product demo mapped to SOL's current workflows

---

## Overview

This demo is structured around SOL's three core operational processes:

1. **QSW & FM Enquiry → Delivery Flow** (end-to-end from enquiry to handover)
2. **Work Order Process** (reactive and project/quote works)
3. **Timesheet Sign Off & Payroll Preparation**

For each process, we show only the StoneRise modules that directly replace or improve what SOL currently does. We do not demo anything outside of these workflows.

---

## Part 1 — Procurement: Replacing the Materials Ordering Process

**SOL's current pain point:**  
Materials are ordered by admin, manually, in line with merchant quotes provided by PMs or via operative links on JotForm. PMs are expected to demonstrate "better buying" (SLA 5) — minimum 2 quotes per subcontractor/merchant. There is no structured system for tracking this.

**What to show in StoneRise Procurement:**

### 1.1 Request Creation
> *"When a PO is received and materials need to be ordered, instead of admin manually emailing merchants, they raise a materials request directly in StoneRise — linked to the job or project code."*

- Show: Create a new **Materials Request**
- Highlight: Job/project code allocation at line-item level — cost goes straight to the right job
- Highlight: Auto-save drafts so nothing gets lost mid-process

### 1.2 RFQ to Multiple Suppliers (Better Buying — SLA 5)
> *"SOL requires a minimum of 2 quotes per merchant. In StoneRise, the request is automatically sent as an RFQ to multiple suppliers at the same time. Quotes come back into the platform — no inbox searching, no spreadsheets."*

- Show: Split a request across 2–3 suppliers using **Intelligent RFQ Allocation**
- Highlight: Line-item level split — different items can go to different suppliers
- Highlight: All quotes land in one place; PM can compare side-by-side

### 1.3 Approval Workflow
> *"Once the PM has selected the best quote, it goes through an approval workflow based on value — the right person approves without the PM having to chase."*

- Show: **Multi-Level Approval Workflow** — value-based routing
- Highlight: Mobile approvals — PM can approve from site
- Highlight: Delegation if approver is unavailable

### 1.4 Purchase Order Generation
> *"Once approved, the PO is auto-generated and sent to the supplier. No manual document creation."*

- Show: Auto-generated PO from approved RFQ
- Highlight: Full audit trail — who approved, when, at what value

### 1.5 Delivery Tracking
> *"When materials arrive on site, the operative confirms delivery in StoneRise. This closes the loop — procurement knows what's been received."*

- Show: **Delivery Tracking** for materials
- Highlight: Quick receipt on mobile — no paperwork

### 1.6 AI Invoice Matching
> *"When the invoice arrives, StoneRise OCR-reads it, matches it to the PO and delivery receipt — three-way match. Any discrepancy is flagged automatically."*

- Show: **AI Invoice Matching** — upload a batch of invoices
- Highlight: Discrepancy management queue — only exceptions need human attention

## Part 2 — HR: Replacing the Timesheet & Payroll Preparation Process

**SOL's current pain point:**  
The timesheet process involves manual downloads from a tracker, manual hour calculations (including travel time with 30-min deductions), Business Support chasing sign-offs via email, a manual approval chain involving Project Managers, and a final export to Sage. The process relies heavily on email, screenshots, and Excel snippets — with multiple manual handoffs and a 48-hour operative review window.

**What to show in StoneRise HR:**

### 2.1 Timesheet Submission (Operative)
> *"Instead of Business Support downloading and calculating hours from a tracker, operatives submit their own timesheets directly in StoneRise from their phone. Travel time rules can be configured — so the 30-minute deduction each way is applied automatically."*

- Show: **Timesheet Submission** on mobile
- Highlight: Project/job code allocation per day
- Highlight: Configurable rules for travel time deductions

### 2.2 Manager Review & Spot Check
> *"The Business Support Manager currently spot-checks timesheets and sends screenshots to the PM for sign-off. In StoneRise, the PM gets a direct notification and reviews the timesheet in the platform — no email chains, no screenshots."*

- Show: **Manager Portal** — team timesheets view
- Highlight: Approval workflow — PM can approve, amend, or query with a reason
- Highlight: Any unapproved overtime is flagged automatically (no manual querying)

### 2.3 Operative Review Window
> *"SOL gives operatives 48 hours to query their timesheet. In StoneRise, operatives get a notification to review their hours — they can raise a query directly in the platform, which goes straight to the PM."*

- Show: Operative notification + query workflow
- Highlight: Full audit trail of any amendments — who changed what and when

### 2.4 Timesheet Lock & Payroll Export
> *"Once approved, the Business Support Manager locks the timesheet. StoneRise exports payroll hours in a format ready for Sage — no manual spreadsheet compilation."*

- Show: Timesheet lock and **Sage-compatible export**
- Highlight: Hours split by project code is included in the export — no separate accounts spreadsheet needed

## Part 3 — Commercial (Preview): Replacing the QSW Enquiry & Quote Process

> **Note:** The Commercial app is in development. This section is a forward-looking preview — position it as "coming soon" and show the roadmap, not live functionality, unless a dev build is available to demo.

**SOL's current pain point:**  
Enquiries are logged manually on Construct and Eque2. Quotes are developed by PMs, reviewed, and submitted to clients. Outstanding quotes are tracked manually. Win/loss data is tracked by HoD separately. Variations during delivery are managed ad-hoc between TL and PMs.

**What StoneRise will handle:**

### 3.1 Enquiry Logging & Quote Development
> *"When an enquiry comes in — whether reactive or QSW — it's logged in StoneRise. The PM builds the quote in the platform, with subcontractor and merchant costs captured against it."*

- Covers: Enquiry logging, site survey linkage, quote development
- Replaces: Construct quotes tab, manual logging

### 3.2 Quote Submission & Follow-Up Tracking
> *"Outstanding quotes are tracked automatically. The system flags anything that hasn't had a client response after 7 days — aligning with SLA 2. Win/loss data is captured per PM and per client."*

- Covers: Quote follow-up (SLA 2), quote volume tracking (SLA 3), win/loss analysis (SLA 3.5)
- Replaces: Manual HoD tracking

### 3.3 PO Receipt & Dispatch
> *"When a PO is received, it's logged in StoneRise and triggers the work order process — directly linked to the procurement and scheduling workflows already shown."*

- Covers: PO logging, status tracking (SLA 4)
- Replaces: Construct order tab

### 3.4 Variations During Delivery
> *"Variations raised during delivery are submitted through StoneRise, reviewed against the original scope, and linked back to the client contract — giving full upstream/downstream visibility."*

- Covers: In-house variation communication (currently TL/PM ad-hoc)
- Replaces: Informal communication

### 3.5 Handover & Invoicing Sign-Off
> *"At handover, the completed works are signed off in StoneRise — replacing the JotForm submission and Eque2/Touchpoint sign-off. This triggers the invoicing workflow."*

- Covers: Handover (SLA 7), financial sign-off
- Replaces: JotForm, Eque2 attachment, Touchpoint sign-off

## Key Messages to Land

1. **Everything in one place** — SOL currently runs across Construct, Eque2, Touchpoint, JotForm, Site Audit Pro, and Excel. StoneRise consolidates this.
2. **Built for construction** — not a generic system adapted for construction. The workflows match how SOL already operates.
3. **Mobile-first** — operatives, PMs, and managers all work from their phones on site. StoneRise is built for this.
4. **Audit trail and accountability** — every approval, amendment, and sign-off is logged. SOL's SLA tracking becomes automatic, not manual.
5. **Sage integration** — payroll and finance exports work with existing Sage setup. No rip-and-replace.
