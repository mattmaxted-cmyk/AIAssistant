## Context & Rationale

This campaign targets niche, high-intent keywords where large platforms (Sage, Oracle, Procore) are not competing, CPCs are materially lower, and the search term maps directly to a specific Certify feature. The trade-off is lower volume. That is intentional.

## Manual CPC Strategy & Monitoring

Manual CPC is used instead of automated bidding for three reasons:
1. Prevents Google inflating bids to spend unspent budget against low-volume keywords
2. Keeps control during the early data-gathering phase before conversion signals exist
3. Avoids broad match creep that automated strategies encourage

**Starting bids:**
- Ad Group 1: £6.00 (above the £4.07 low range to secure top-of-page placement)
- Ad Group 2: £5.00 (above the £4.23 low range; competition is very low so £5 should dominate)

**Monitoring schedule:**

| Timeframe | Action |
|---|---|
| Days 1–10 | No changes. Gather baseline impression share and avg CPC data. |
| Week 2 review | Check actual avg CPC vs manual bid. If actual CPC is £3–4, lower bid to just above actual. If impression share is below 40%, raise bid by £1 increments. |
| Monthly | Review search terms report. Add irrelevant queries as negatives. Assess CPL vs target. |
| 30-day checkpoint | If CPL is below £150, consider increasing budget. If CPL is above £400, review landing page conversion rate before touching bids. |

**Key signals to watch:**
- Search Impression Share — target 60%+ on both ad groups
- Avg CPC vs manual bid — if actual is well below the manual bid, lower it
- Search terms report — add negatives weekly in first month

### General Quality Score rules
- Phrase match only — no broad match keywords in either ad group
- No keyword crossover between ad groups — each keyword belongs to one group only
- Search terms report reviewed weekly — irrelevant queries added as negatives immediately
- One dedicated landing page per ad group — no sending both groups to the same page
- Do not add keywords to an ad group unless that ad group's landing page directly answers the search intent
