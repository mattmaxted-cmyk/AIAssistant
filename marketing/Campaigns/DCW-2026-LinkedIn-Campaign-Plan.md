## Strategic Rationale

### Why Pre-Event Awareness?

1. **Cold booth visits convert poorly** — attendees walk past stands they don't recognise
2. **Pre-awareness creates recognition** — "I've seen them on LinkedIn" triggers curiosity
3. **Increases stand traffic and meeting bookings** — prospects seek you out rather than stumble across you
4. **DCW has 9,000+ attendees** — you're competing with hundreds of exhibitors for attention

### Why LinkedIn Only?

- Our ICP lives on LinkedIn — Commercial Directors, Finance Directors, Procurement Managers, MDs
- 80% of B2B decision-makers use LinkedIn
- Concentrating budget avoids dilution and gives the algorithm more data
- Meta and Google Search campaigns are already running separately for retargeting and intent capture

## Campaign Structure

### Single Campaign Approach

We're running **one continuous campaign** for the full 4 weeks. This avoids resetting LinkedIn's learning phase mid-campaign.

| Setting | Value |
|---------|-------|
| **Campaign Objective** | Brand Awareness |
| **Optimisation Goal** | Reach (maximise unique impressions) |
| **Bid Strategy** | Maximum Delivery (automated) |
| **Placements** | LinkedIn Feed only (disable Audience Network) |
| **Budget Type** | Daily budget: £270 |
| **Schedule** | 5 May 2026 – 4 June 2026 |
| **Frequency Cap** | 4 impressions per person per week (if available) |

### Creative Rotation (Within Same Campaign)

Rather than separate phases, we rotate creative within the same campaign:

| Period | Dates | Creative Focus | Action |
|--------|-------|----------------|--------|
| **Weeks 1–2** | 5–18 May | Problem/brand awareness | Launch 3 ad variations |
| **Weeks 3–4** | 19 May – 4 June | Event push (DCW) | Add 2–3 new ads; keep top performer from weeks 1–2 |

**Key Rule:** Don't pause week 1–2 ads abruptly. Let them compete with new creative; pause only if CTR drops below 0.3%.
