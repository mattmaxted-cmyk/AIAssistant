## The Two Guides

### Guide A: The Commercial Control Playbook

**Full Title:** The Commercial Control Playbook: Payments, Variations and Approvals Done Right

**Target Persona:** Commercial Directors, QS Directors, Head of Commercial at UK main contractors

**Key Topics:**
- Payment applications — getting every penny you're owed
- Final account strategy — recovering what you're owed at settlement
- Cash flow forecasting — why numbers diverge from reality
- Subcontractor over-claiming — building an assessment process that catches it
- Variations — how contractors lose money they were entitled to
- Approval workflows — when bottlenecks become business risk
- Audit trails — why documentation wins or loses disputes

**Key Stats from Guide:**
- £26bn overdue payments owed to UK businesses at any given time
- 2,100+ construction firms closed in 2024 due to late payment
- 50% of adjudicated disputes attributed to inadequate contract administration
- 3–7% of project value destroyed by poor commercial management on average
- Variations represent 10–20% of final account value
- Late notification is the #1 reason contractors fail to recover variation costs
- 63 days average payment time reported by some large UK contractors
- Adjudication costs typically £20,000–£50,000

**Hooks to Use:**
- "Variations represent 10–20% of final account value. The number one reason contractors don't recover them? Late notification."
- "Miss a pay less notice deadline and the full application amount becomes due. No deductions. No comeback."
- "Poor commercial management destroys 3–7% of project value on average."
- "The difference between 8% margin and 2% margin isn't the contract price — it's whether you caught the variation in time."

### Guide B: Construction in 2026: The Risks UK Contractors Can't Afford to Ignore

**Full Title:** Construction in 2026: The Risks UK Contractors Can't Afford to Ignore

**Target Persona:** Managing Directors, Finance Directors, Commercial Leaders at UK main contractors

**Key Topics:**
- Rising costs compressing margins on fixed-price contracts
- New legislation (NI increase, minimum wage, Building Safety Levy, Part B staircases)
- Industry insolvency rate at crisis levels
- Talent pipeline breaking down
- Poor internal processes costing more than you think
- Competitors starting to adopt technology

**Key Stats from Guide:**
- 3.6% forecast construction cost increase in 2026
- Employer NI up to 15%, threshold down to £5,000
- £926 extra NI cost per employee on £35k salary per year
- 17.1% of all UK insolvencies are in construction (January 2026)
- 3,912 construction firms became insolvent in 12 months to January 2026
- 4th consecutive year construction has highest insolvency rate
- 140,000+ construction job vacancies remain unfilled
- Only 12% of UK construction firms have meaningful technology deployment

**Hooks to Use:**
- "2026 brings a specific combination of pressures that represent one of the most challenging operating environments the industry has faced in years."
- "17.1% of all company insolvencies in England and Wales are in construction."
- "Over 140,000 construction job vacancies remain unfilled."
- "Only 12% of UK construction firms have deployed technology meaningfully."

## Notes for Designer

1. **Native UI formats (Notes App, WhatsApp) must look authentic** — these work because they don't look like ads. Over-designing them defeats the purpose.

2. **Text must be large and readable** — LinkedIn users scroll fast. If the headline isn't readable in 1 second, it won't stop the scroll.

3. **Keep it simple** — the whitepaper/book creatives should feel professional but not cluttered. One hero element (the book), one stat if included, logo, badge. That's it.

4. **Consistency across the set** — all 10 creatives should feel like they're from the same brand, even with different formats.

5. **The myth-busting format is text-led** — don't add imagery. The power is in the quote and the counter-statement.
