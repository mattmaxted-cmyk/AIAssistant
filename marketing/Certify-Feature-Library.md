# StoneRise Certify — Feature Library

Reference document for marketing, social media, and product content related to the Certify module (payment applications).

---

## What is Certify?

Certify is StoneRise's payment application and certification platform, purpose-built for construction main contractors and subcontractors. It manages the full lifecycle of payment applications — from initial submission through valuation, certification, and payment — both client-facing and subcontractor-facing.

---

## Feature Definitions

### 1. Automatic Payment Timeline Generation

**What it does:**
When a contract is set up in Certify, the system automatically generates the complete payment timeline for both the main contract (client-facing) and any subcontracts (subcontractor-facing). This includes all application dates, valuation dates, certification deadlines, and final payment dates for the full contract duration.

**Why it matters technically:**
Construction contracts require strict adherence to payment timelines under the Construction Act. Missing dates — even by one day — can result in mandatory payment of disputed sums or loss of withholding rights. Manual calendar management across multiple contracts and subcontractors creates serious legal and financial risk.

**Pain point addressed:** Missed payment deadlines, legal exposure, admin overhead
**ICP relevance:** Commercial Leaders, Finance Leaders, Operations Leaders

---

### 2. Dual Approval for Variations

**What it does:**
Variations in Certify require agreement from both the main contractor and the subcontractor before they are formally approved and applied to the contract. Neither party can unilaterally push through a variation — both sides must accept the scope and value.

**Why it matters technically:**
Disputed variations are the most common source of construction payment disputes and final account disagreements. Without mutual sign-off, variations get applied informally (email or verbal agreement), leading to contested final accounts. Dual approval creates a clear, contractually defensible record that both parties accepted the change.

**Pain point addressed:** Disputed valuations, lack of audit trail, final account disputes
**ICP relevance:** Commercial Leaders, Finance Leaders

---

### 3. Locked Contract Value

**What it does:**
The main contract value is locked at the point of contract award. It cannot be changed except through the formal variation process — each approved variation adjusts the contract sum with a full audit trail.

**Why it matters technically:**
Without a locked contract value, commercial teams lose track of the true contract sum as informal changes accumulate. This creates gaps between what was agreed, what was built, and what was claimed — one of the primary causes of margin erosion and final account disputes.

**Pain point addressed:** Margin erosion, uncontrolled contract creep, audit trail gaps
**ICP relevance:** Commercial Leaders, Finance Leaders, Operations Leaders

---

### 4. Full Approval Workflow Builder

**What it does:**
Certify includes a configurable approval workflow builder that allows businesses to route payment applications, variations, and certifications to specific job roles. Workflows can be built visually, with clear routing rules, escalation paths, and sign-off requirements tailored to the company's structure.

**Why it matters technically:**
In construction businesses, approval chains vary significantly by company size, project value, and contract type. Off-the-shelf systems force everyone through the same approval path. A configurable builder ensures that every action is reviewed by the right person — nothing falls through the cracks because it went to the wrong inbox or was skipped in a busy period.

**Pain point addressed:** Items missed in approval chains, wrong people signing off, lack of process control
**ICP relevance:** Commercial Leaders, Operations Leaders

---

### 5. Full Subcontractor Payment Visibility

**What it does:**
Certify provides a complete real-time view of every subcontractor payment across all active projects — what has been submitted, what has been certified, what has been paid, and what remains outstanding. Nothing is hidden in email threads or separate spreadsheets.

**Why it matters technically:**
On a mid-size main contractor with 15 to 30 active subcontractors across multiple projects, tracking payment status is typically fragmented across project QS spreadsheets. Commercial directors and FDs have no centralised view without manually chasing teams. This creates both cash flow surprises and compliance risk around the Construction Act's payment obligations.

**Pain point addressed:** No commercial visibility across projects, cash flow surprises, spreadsheet chaos
**ICP relevance:** Finance Leaders, Operations Leaders, Commercial Leaders

## Competitor Context

Certify is positioned as the construction-native alternative to tools like PayApps and similar payment application platforms. Unlike standalone payment platforms, Certify sits within the full StoneRise ERP — meaning variation data, procurement costs, and commercial data are all connected in one system.
