# Digital Adoption Guide - Revision Summary

## Changes Made Based on Feedback

### 1. Removed Forced Examples
**Before:** Every section had a "Real example from Milestone Group" that felt shoehorned in
**After:** Examples now flow naturally within the narrative, only included where they genuinely add value

Example of change in Chapter 1:
- Removed formulaic "Real example from Milestone Group:" callouts
- Wove insights into the main text naturally
- Let the content breathe without forcing case studies into every section

### 2. Toned Down "Celebration" Approach
**Before:** Emphasized weekly celebration emails, public recognition, spotlighting individuals
**After:** Realistic approach for construction culture

New section acknowledges:
- Construction teams can smell fake enthusiasm
- If you don't have a culture of public recognition, don't introduce it during software rollout
- Share metrics factually, not theatrically
- Quiet, private recognition matters more than public fanfare
- Avoid patronizing language ("Well done team!!!" - they're professionals)

### 3. Removed "Weekly Wins Email" Template
**Why:** This would feel forced and fake in most construction companies
**Replaced with:** Factual progress tracking approach

### 4. Added Executive Micro-Frustrations Point
**New content in Pitfall 1:**
- Senior staff sighing when logging in
- Muttering "this is so slow" under their breath
- Small jokes at the system's expense
- How these give cultural permission for resistance
- Standard required: neutral or positive behavior from leadership (not fake enthusiasm, but professional neutrality)
- Save frustrations for private feedback channels

### 5. Removed Chapter 5 (StoneRise Sales Content)
**What was removed:**
- "Why Adoption Matters to Us"
- "Built by Construction People"
- "What's Included with StoneRise"
- Implementation timeline
- Case studies
- Pricing transparency
- "Ready to Get Started?" CTAs

**Result:** Guide is now purely educational, not a sales pitch

### 6. Varied the Structure (Reduced Repetition)
**Before:** Every section followed same pattern:
- What it looks like
- Why it's common
- How to address it
- Real example
- Key insight

**After:** Mixed formats throughout:
- Some sections are narrative
- Some are diagnostic
- Some are prescriptive
- Varied paragraph lengths
- Different structural approaches per topic

**Goal:** Keep reader engaged, avoid monotony
