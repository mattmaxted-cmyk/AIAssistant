## What is Three-Way Matching?

Three-way matching is a verification process that compares three documents before approving a supplier invoice for payment:

1. **Purchase Order (PO):** What you ordered
2. **Goods Received Note (GRN) / Delivery Note:** What was actually delivered
3. **Invoice:** What the supplier is charging you

The goal is simple: only pay for what you ordered *and* received, at the price you agreed.

### How the Three Documents Connect

| Document | What It Shows | Who Creates It |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Purchase Order** | Items ordered, quantities, agreed prices, delivery date | Your procurement/buying team |
| **Delivery Note / GRN** | Items delivered, quantities received, condition, date | Site team (confirms receipt) |
| **Invoice** | Items billed, quantities charged, prices, payment terms | Supplier |

When all three documents align (same items, same quantities, same prices) the invoice is approved for payment. When they don't match, you've caught an error before it costs you money.

## Why Three-Way Matching Matters in Construction

Construction procurement has unique characteristics that make three-way matching particularly important:

### 1. High Invoice Volume

A mid-sized contractor might process 300-500 purchase orders per month across multiple projects. Each PO can generate multiple deliveries and invoices. Without a systematic matching process, errors slip through.

### 2. Partial Deliveries Are Common

Construction materials rarely arrive in a single delivery. A concrete order might come in multiple pours. Timber arrives in batches. Plant hire runs across variable periods. Each partial delivery needs tracking against the original order.

### 3. Site Conditions Change

What gets ordered isn't always what's needed by the time it arrives. Variations, design changes, and site conditions mean quantities frequently change. Three-way matching catches when invoices don't reflect these changes.

### 4. Multiple Stakeholders

The person ordering materials (site manager or buyer) isn't the same person receiving them (site operative) or paying for them (accounts team). Three-way matching creates accountability across these handoffs.

### 5. Tight Margins

Construction operates on thin margins, typically 2-5% net profit according to [Build UK industry benchmarks](https://builduk.org/). Overpaying on invoices by even 1-2% can wipe out project profitability. Three-way matching protects those margins.

## Common Problems Three-Way Matching Prevents

### Duplicate Invoices

Suppliers occasionally send the same invoice twice, or resubmit after a system error. Without matching to delivery records, you might pay both.

### Quantity Discrepancies

The invoice says 500 bricks, but only 450 were delivered. Without checking the delivery note, you overpay by 10%.

### Price Variations

The PO agreed £12.50 per bag of cement, but the invoice charges £13.75. Small differences across hundreds of line items add up quickly.

### Goods Not Received

An invoice arrives for materials that never made it to site. Lost in transit, delivered to the wrong project, or simply never dispatched.

### Incorrect Project Coding

Materials ordered for Project A get invoiced against Project B. Without matching, your project cost reports become unreliable.

## The Manual Three-Way Matching Process

Many construction firms still do three-way matching manually. Here's what that typically looks like:

### Step 1: Receive the Invoice

The supplier sends an invoice, often by email or post. Someone in accounts logs it and assigns it to a project.

### Step 2: Find the Purchase Order

The accounts team searches for the corresponding PO. This might mean digging through email chains, shared drives, or a basic PO log spreadsheet.

### Step 3: Locate the Delivery Note

Next, they need proof the goods arrived. This requires chasing site teams for signed delivery notes, often scanned photos, WhatsApp messages, or physical paperwork posted back to the office.

### Step 4: Compare All Three

With all three documents in hand, someone manually compares:
- Do the items match?
- Do the quantities match?
- Do the prices match?
- Is the delivery note signed and dated?

### Step 5: Resolve Discrepancies

If anything doesn't match, the accounts team must investigate. This means calling suppliers, chasing site managers, and going back through records.

### Step 6: Approve or Query

Once matched (or discrepancies resolved), the invoice is approved for payment or formally queried with the supplier.

### The Problem with Manual Matching

This process is slow, error-prone, and doesn't scale:

- **Time-consuming:** Each invoice can take 15-30 minutes to fully match
- **Inconsistent:** Different people apply different standards
- **Incomplete records:** Delivery notes get lost or never make it back from site
- **No audit trail:** Difficult to prove what was checked and when
- **Bottlenecks:** Month-end becomes a scramble to clear the backlog

## Automated Three-Way Matching

Modern [construction procurement software](/products/procurement) automates the entire three-way matching process. Here's how it works:

### Digital Purchase Orders

Every order is created in the system with full line-item detail: quantities, prices, specifications, and project codes. No more hunting for paperwork.

### Mobile Delivery Confirmation

When goods arrive on site, the operative confirms receipt on their phone. They can:
- Check off delivered items against the PO
- Record actual quantities received
- Flag damaged or missing items
- Capture photos as evidence
- Add delivery notes or comments

This creates a digital Goods Received Note instantly, linked to the original PO.

### AI-Powered Invoice Processing

When supplier invoices arrive (email, PDF upload, or integration), AI extracts the line items, quantities, and prices automatically. The system then:

1. Matches the invoice to the correct PO
2. Compares invoice quantities to confirmed deliveries
3. Checks prices against agreed PO rates
4. Flags any discrepancies for review

### Exception-Based Workflow

Instead of checking every invoice manually, your team only reviews exceptions:

- **Full match:** Invoice auto-approved (or queued for batch approval)
- **Minor variance:** Flagged for quick review (e.g., 2% price difference)
- **Major discrepancy:** Blocked until resolved (e.g., invoiced for undelivered goods)

This reduces invoice processing time from 15-30 minutes to under 3 minutes.

## Three-Way Matching vs Two-Way Matching

Some businesses use two-way matching, comparing just the PO and invoice without the delivery confirmation.

### When Two-Way Matching Works

- Low-risk, low-value purchases
- Services rather than physical goods
- Trusted suppliers with strong track records

### Why Construction Needs Three-Way

For construction procurement, two-way matching isn't enough:

- Materials get delivered to the wrong site
- Quantities vary from what was ordered
- Partial deliveries are the norm
- Site conditions cause rejections and returns

The delivery confirmation is essential. Without it, you're trusting that everything ordered was received correctly. That's a risky assumption on a busy construction site.

## FAQ: Three-Way Matching in Construction

### What documents are used in three-way matching?

Three-way matching compares three documents: the purchase order (what you ordered), the goods received note or delivery note (what was delivered), and the supplier invoice (what you're being charged). All three must align before payment is approved.

### How long does three-way matching take?

Manual three-way matching typically takes 15-30 minutes per invoice, including time to locate documents, compare details, and resolve discrepancies. Automated matching software can reduce this to under 3 minutes.

### What happens when documents don't match?

When discrepancies are found (different quantities, prices, or missing deliveries), the invoice is flagged for investigation. The finance team must determine whether it's a supplier error, a receiving error, or a legitimate variance before approving or querying the invoice.

### Is three-way matching required by law?

Three-way matching isn't legally required, but it's considered best practice for financial controls. Many construction contracts, [ISO 9001 quality management certifications](https://www.iso.org/iso-9001-quality-management.html), and audit standards expect evidence of systematic invoice verification.

### Can three-way matching be automated?

Yes. Modern procurement software automates three-way matching by digitising purchase orders, enabling mobile delivery confirmation, and using AI to extract and match invoice data. This reduces manual effort by 80% or more.
