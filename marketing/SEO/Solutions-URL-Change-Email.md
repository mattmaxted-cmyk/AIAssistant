The HR page is showing some good early SEO signals. It's picking up impressions for construction HR keywords which means Google is already recognising it as relevant. No significant clicks yet as the rankings are still building, but that's expected at this stage. The plan is to back it up with targeted blog posts and backlinks to push it up the rankings and convert those impressions into clicks. It's the first real proof that the approach is working.

The reason it's performing is the URL. `/solutions/construction-hr` contains the keyword people are searching for, and Google picks that up as a relevance signal. The other Solutions pages don't have this yet.

I need Michael to make the following URL changes and set up 301 redirects for each:

| Page | Current URL | New URL |
|---|---|---|
| Procurement | `/solutions/procurement` | `/solutions/construction-procurement-software` |
| Payments | `/solutions/payments` | `/solutions/construction-payment-software` |
| Health & Safety | `/solutions/health-and-safety` | `/solutions/construction-health-safety-software` |
| Site Management | `/solutions/site-management` | `/solutions/construction-site-management-software` |
| HR | `/solutions/construction-hr` | No change |

The 301 redirects are important. They tell Google the page has moved permanently and pass any existing ranking value to the new URL. Without them, the old URLs effectively become dead pages and we lose whatever ground we have.
