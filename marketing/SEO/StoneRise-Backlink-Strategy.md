## Executive Overview

### About StoneRise

StoneRise is a **comprehensive ERP platform purpose-built for construction**—the only solution addressing the complete construction business lifecycle. The platform includes:

- **Procurement** (live) — requests, RFQs, POs, delivery tracking, AI invoice matching
- **HR** (live) — employee management, leave, expenses, LMS, training records
- **Commercial/QS** (in development) — variations, payment applications, subcontractor management
- **Health & Safety** (planned) — RAMS, permits, audits, incident reporting
- **Site Management** (planned) — quality control, diaries, progress tracking
- Plus: Fleet, CRM, Asset Management, Timesheets, Labour Gang apps

**Key differentiator:** Built by construction professionals (Stelios and Michael are qualified QSs who ran construction businesses for ~10 years).

### The Goal

Build StoneRise's domain authority to rank for construction ERP and operational keywords. Current priority is establishing a foundation of quality backlinks to compete for Tier 3-4 keywords, with a path to Tier 1 commercial keywords over time.

## Part 3: Guest Post Outreach

### 3.1 Branded Author Bylines (Tier 1 Keyword Strategy)

For Tier 1 commercial keywords like "construction ERP software" or "construction management software UK", it's extremely difficult to rank guest posts directly. Instead, we use **branded author bylines** to build keyword association and earn high-authority backlinks.

**How it works:**
1. Michael or Stelios writes an educational guest piece (doesn't need to mention software)
2. The publication includes an author bio with company positioning and a link
3. The byline creates brand + category association for readers and search engines
4. The DA 50+ backlink boosts StoneRise's domain authority

**Example bylines:**

> *Michael Loizias is Co-founder and Chief Product Officer at [StoneRise](https://stonerise.co.uk), a construction management platform built for UK contractors. A qualified Quantity Surveyor, Michael spent 10 years running construction businesses before building StoneRise to solve the operational challenges he experienced firsthand.*

> *Stelios Ioannou is CEO of [StoneRise](https://stonerise.co.uk), the construction ERP platform helping UK contractors streamline operations. A qualified QS with a decade of hands-on construction experience, Stelios now focuses on bringing modern technology to an industry often underserved by software.*

**Byline variations by topic:**

| Guest Post Topic | Byline Positioning |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Procurement / purchasing | "...StoneRise, construction procurement and ERP software..." |
| Commercial / QS | "...StoneRise, construction management software for commercial teams..." |
| Finance / cash flow | "...StoneRise, the construction ERP platform helping contractors..." |
| Operations / scaling | "...StoneRise, the all-in-one construction management platform..." |
| HR / training | "...StoneRise, construction operations software including HR..." |

**What to request from publications:**
1. Full author bio (not just "Michael Loizias, StoneRise")
2. Dofollow link to stonerise.co.uk
3. Specific anchor text where possible ("StoneRise" or "construction management software")

**Why this works:**
- You're not trying to rank the guest post for "construction ERP software"
- The SEO value comes from the high-DA backlink boosting your domain
- Readers see "StoneRise" + "construction management software" together repeatedly
- Being published in CIOB, Construction News, etc. builds credibility
- Your own blog posts targeting Tier 1 keywords rank better because of the authority

## Part 4: Link Quality Standards

### Accept Links From:
- DA/DR 25+ sites
- Relevant niches: construction, finance, business, technology, SME, HR, health & safety
- Editorial content (articles, blogs, news)
- Dofollow links (nofollow acceptable from very high DA sites)

### Avoid:
- DA/DR below 20
- Generic business directories
- Sites with obvious link farms or spam
- Irrelevant niches (health, lifestyle, fashion, etc.)
- Paid link schemes that violate Google guidelines

### Link Value Reference

| Link Source DA | Equivalent Value |
|----------------|------------------|
| DA 50+ (CIOB, RICS) | = 8-12 links from DA 25 sites |
| DA 40-49 | = 4-6 links from DA 25 sites |
| DA 30-39 | = 2-3 links from DA 25 sites |
| DA 20-29 | = Baseline value |
| DA <20 | = Minimal value |
