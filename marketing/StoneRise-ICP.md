# StoneRise Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

This document defines StoneRise's Ideal Customer Profile and buyer personas. Use this as the single source of truth for all targeting decisions across LinkedIn organic content, paid advertising, PhantomBuster automation, Sales Navigator searches, and HubSpot segmentation.

---

## Core ICP Definition

### Company Profile

| Attribute | Ideal | Acceptable |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| **Geography** | United Kingdom | UK-based with UK operations |
| **Industry** | Construction main contractors, subcontractors | Any built environment contractor |
| **Turnover** | £10m – £50m | £5m – £100m |
| **Employees** | 50 – 200 | 11 – 500 |
| **Monthly PO Volume** | 200+ | 100+ |
| **Concurrent Projects** | 5+ | 3+ |

### Industry Sectors (Include)

- Building contractors (residential, commercial, mixed-use)
- Civil engineering contractors
- Roofing contractors
- M&E contractors
- Groundworks contractors
- Fit-out and refurbishment contractors
- Specialist subcontractors with material procurement
- Any contractor with subcontractor management needs

### Industry Sectors (Exclude)

- Sole traders / one-man bands
- Pure labour-only subcontractors (no material procurement)
- Housebuilders with in-house ERP (Barratt, Persimmon, etc.)
- Tier 1 contractors with enterprise systems (Balfour Beatty, Skanska, etc.)
- Non-UK construction businesses

## Buyer Personas

StoneRise has **four distinct buyer personas** within target companies. Each has different pain points, motivations, and decision authority.

### Persona 1: Commercial Leader

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Job Titles** | Commercial Director, QS Director, Head of Commercial, Chief Quantity Surveyor, Commercial Manager (senior) |
| **Function** | Commercial / Quantity Surveying |
| **Seniority** | Director / Head of |
| **Reports To** | MD / Operations Director |
| **Team Size** | Manages 2-15 QSs |

**Primary Pain Points:**
- Spreadsheet chaos — CVRs, final accounts, variations all in Excel
- No real-time cost visibility — month-end reconciliation surprises
- Margin erosion from untracked variations and scope creep
- Subcontractor payment disputes and application chaos
- QS team buried in admin instead of commercial management
- No audit trail for variation decisions

**Buying Motivation:**
- Protect project margins
- Reduce QS administrative burden
- One source of truth for commercial data
- Real-time cost position (not month-end)
- Defensible audit trail for disputes

**Decision Authority:** High — often final decision maker for commercial tools

**Key Metrics They Care About:**
- Project margin (%)
- Variation recovery rate
- Time to final account
- QS hours per £m turnover

---

### Persona 2: Finance Leader

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Job Titles** | Finance Director, FD, Financial Controller, CFO, Head of Finance |
| **Function** | Finance / Accounts |
| **Seniority** | Director / C-Suite |
| **Reports To** | MD / Board |
| **Team Size** | Manages 2-10 finance staff |

**Primary Pain Points:**
- Cash flow uncertainty — don't know true committed costs
- Invoice disputes and payment errors (duplicate payments, missed credits)
- Reconciliation burden between systems
- No confidence invoices match what was ordered/delivered
- Month-end close takes too long
- Audit trail gaps for compliance

**Buying Motivation:**
- Control financial leakage
- Improve cash flow forecasting
- Reduce reconciliation time
- Complete audit trail
- Confidence in payment accuracy

**Decision Authority:** High — budget holder, controls spend approval

**Key Metrics They Care About:**
- Payment error rate (%)
- Days to month-end close
- Cash flow accuracy
- Finance team hours on reconciliation

---

### Persona 3: Operations Leader

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Job Titles** | Managing Director, MD, Operations Director, CEO, Owner, Founder |
| **Function** | Executive / Operations |
| **Seniority** | C-Suite / Owner |
| **Reports To** | Board / Shareholders |
| **Team Size** | Oversees entire operation |

**Primary Pain Points:**
- Scaling without proportional overhead growth
- Admin bottlenecks slowing project delivery
- No single view of business performance
- Dependent on key individuals for information
- Competitive pressure on margins
- Multiple disconnected systems

**Buying Motivation:**
- Efficiency at scale — grow revenue without growing back office
- Competitive advantage through better operations
- Reduce dependency on individuals
- Strategic visibility across projects
- Protect margins as business grows

**Decision Authority:** Highest — ultimate sign-off on significant purchases

**Key Metrics They Care About:**
- Overhead as % of turnover
- Revenue per employee
- Project profitability consistency
- Time from tender to final account

---

### Persona 4: Procurement Leader

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Job Titles** | Procurement Manager, Procurement Director, Head of Procurement, Buying Manager, Buyer, Senior Buyer |
| **Function** | Procurement / Buying |
| **Seniority** | Manager / Director / Head of |
| **Reports To** | Commercial Director / Operations Director / MD |
| **Team Size** | 1-10 procurement/buying staff |

**Primary Pain Points:**
- Manual processes — email, phone, spreadsheets consuming team capacity
- Chasing quotes from suppliers
- No visibility on supplier performance or spend analytics
- Approval delays holding up orders and projects
- No centralised supplier document management (insurances, accreditations)
- Repeat data entry across systems
- Difficulty demonstrating procurement value to the business
- No leverage for supplier negotiations (lack of spend data)

**Buying Motivation:**
- Day-to-day efficiency for the team
- Strategic procurement capability (spend analysis, supplier performance)
- Supplier visibility and comparison
- Faster approvals
- One place for supplier information
- Demonstrate ROI of procurement function to leadership

**Decision Authority:** Medium to High — often budget holder for procurement tools; may need MD/FD sign-off for larger investments

**Key Metrics They Care About:**
- Time per PO
- Cost savings achieved
- Supplier response time
- Spend under management
- Approval turnaround time
- Supplier compliance rates

## Persona Priority

| Priority | Persona | Reason |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **1** | Commercial Leader | Primary user of Commercial App; high decision authority; feels pain most acutely |
| **2** | Finance Leader | Budget holder; ROI resonates strongly; controls spend approval |
| **3** | Procurement Leader | Core user of Procurement App; medium-high decision authority; direct beneficiary of efficiency gains |
| **4** | Operations Leader | Ultimate sign-off; cares about scale and efficiency; harder to reach directly |

## Lead Qualification Criteria

When filtering scraped LinkedIn engagers or inbound leads, use this checklist:

| Check | Required | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| UK-based | Yes | Confirm UK geography |
| Construction company | Yes | Main contractor or subcontractor |
| Company size 10+ employees | Yes | Minimum viable customer |
| Decision-maker or influencer | Yes | Director/senior role in Commercial, Finance, or Operations |
| Not a competitor | Yes | Not software vendor, consultant, or recruiter |
| Not existing contact | Yes | Check against HubSpot CRM |
| Monthly PO volume 100+ | Preferred | May need qualification call to confirm |
