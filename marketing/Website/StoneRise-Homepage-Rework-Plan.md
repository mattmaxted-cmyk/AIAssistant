## Executive Summary

### The Problem

The current StoneRise homepage positions the product as **procurement software only**. This:

- Undersells the platform (we have HR, Commercial/QS, and multiple planned modules)
- Limits organic search opportunity (misses "construction ERP" keywords)
- Creates positioning confusion when prospects discover the full product suite
- Doesn't reflect where the product is going (complete business lifecycle)

### The Solution

Reposition the homepage around **"ERP for Construction Subcontractors"** — a complete business platform built specifically for the construction industry, with procurement as one (powerful) module among many.

### Key Changes

| Current | New |
|---------|-----|
| "Construction Procurement Software" | "Construction ERP / Business Platform" |
| Single-use-case focus | Multi-module platform |
| Procurement teams only | All personas (Commercial, Finance, Operations, Procurement) |
| Feature-led messaging | Outcome-led messaging |

---

## Current State Analysis

### Current Positioning Issues

1. **Too Narrow:** Homepage focuses exclusively on procurement, ignoring HR (live), Commercial (in development), and planned modules
2. **Wrong Category:** "Procurement software" is a smaller search category than "construction ERP" or "construction software"
3. **Limited Audience:** Current messaging speaks mainly to Procurement Leaders, missing Commercial Leaders, Finance Leaders, and Operations Leaders
4. **Point Solution Perception:** Visitors may see StoneRise as a tool, not a platform — limiting expansion potential within accounts

### What's Working

- Clear value proposition for procurement (keep the specificity)
- Construction-specific language (maintain industry authenticity)
- Proof points are concrete (3 minutes, 20%, 90%, 75%, 90%)
- Problem-first messaging structure (retain this approach)

## Trust & Credibility Framework

### Trust Elements by Section

| Section | Trust Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| Hero | "Built by Chartered QSs" badge | Immediate credibility |
| Logo Bar | Customer/partner logos | Social proof |
| Problem | Industry-specific language | "They understand us" |
| Module Grid | "Live" status badges | Product maturity |
| Product Visual | Real screenshot | Proof it exists |
| Social Proof | Named testimonial | Third-party validation |
| Differentiators | Founder backgrounds | Expertise trust |
| Proof Points | Specific numbers | Evidence of results |
| FAQ | Detailed answers | Transparency |
| Footer | Company details | Legitimacy |

### Building Trust for a New Visitor

A first-time visitor will subconsciously ask:

1. **"What is this?"** → Hero answers in 5 seconds
2. **"Is this for me?"** → Subheadline + Problem section confirms fit
3. **"Does it actually work?"** → Product visual + testimonial proves it
4. **"Can I trust these people?"** → Differentiators + founder credibility
5. **"What does it cost?"** → FAQ or pricing link provides transparency
6. **"What's the next step?"** → CTAs make action obvious

## Copy Guidelines

### Voice & Tone

| Attribute | Guidance |
|-----------|----------|
| **Confident** | State things directly, don't hedge |
| **Specific** | Use numbers, name features, be concrete |
| **Construction-native** | Use industry terms naturally (PO, RFQ, subbie, variations, retention) |
| **Problem-first** | Lead with pain, follow with solution |
| **No hype** | Avoid "revolutionary", "game-changing", "best-in-class" |

### Words to Use

- ERP, business platform, construction software
- Procurement, commercial, HR, operations
- Subcontractors, contractors, QS, quantity surveyor
- Variations, payment applications, CIS, retention
- Visibility, control, audit trail
- Real-time, automated, integrated

### Words to Avoid

- Revolutionary, game-changing, cutting-edge
- Solution (overused — be specific)
- Platform (when you mean specific module)
- Seamless (means nothing)
- Best-in-class (unverifiable)

### Messaging Hierarchy (for any section)

1. Lead with the problem or outcome
2. Introduce StoneRise as the category answer
3. Name the specific capability
4. Prove with a stat or example
5. Close with differentiation or CTA

### Key Differentiators

1. **Built by construction professionals** — Stelios and Michael are qualified QSs who ran construction businesses for ~10 years
2. **No translation loss** — Michael actively scopes and develops the software; speaks construction, technical, coding, and business languages
3. **Modern tech stack** — Not an outdated legacy system
4. **Bespoke QS AI** — Training own model specifically for quantity surveying, not plug-and-play
5. **Complete lifecycle** — Only solution covering the entire construction business lifecycle

### AI Capabilities

**QS AI Model (Bespoke):**
- Downstream Variation Assessment
- Upstream Variation Generation
- Contract Document Analysis (JCT, NEC, bespoke)
- Pricing Intelligence
- Measurement & Quantification
