### 2. Problem Block — 3 Pain Points
Present as short, punchy statements. Each should feel like something the reader recognises from their own day:

- When approvals happen over WhatsApp or email, the business has no enforceable spend control. The same order gets approved at site level that should have gone to a director, and nobody finds out until the invoice arrives
- There is no escalation when an approver does not respond. Orders get delayed waiting for someone to come back from site, and the business either waits or bypasses the process entirely
- Without a record of who approved what and when, audits and disputes are impossible to defend. The authorisation exists somewhere in a chat thread, but finding it is not a realistic option

### 3. Solution Block — How StoneRise Procurement Works
Four features, each as a heading plus one sentence of benefit:

- Value-based approval routing — approval chains are configured by spend value, so a £500 order routes to one approver and a £50,000 order routes to a different one, automatically, without anyone having to decide
- Mobile approvals — approvers receive a notification and can approve or reject directly from their phone, so site-based managers are not a bottleneck in the process
- Delegation — when an approver is unavailable, authority can be delegated to a nominated substitute for a set period, ensuring orders are never held up unnecessarily
- Full approval audit trail — every request, approval decision and comment is recorded against the order, creating an unambiguous record that survives any dispute or audit
