### 2. Problem Block — 3 Pain Points
Present as short, punchy statements. Each should feel like something the reader recognises from their own day:

- Supplier contact details, insurance documents and trade accreditations are held in spreadsheets, email inboxes and individual laptops across the business. There is no single version that is current, complete and accessible to everyone who needs it
- Without a record of supplier performance, the same companies get awarded orders regardless of how they performed last time. Late deliveries, wrong materials and short quantities happen repeatedly because there is no data to justify a change
- Insurance certificates and trade accreditations expire without anyone noticing. Orders go out to suppliers who are no longer compliant because nobody has a system that tracks the dates

### 3. Solution Block — How StoneRise Procurement Works
Four features, each as a heading plus one sentence of benefit:

- Central supplier directory — every supplier is held in a single platform-wide register with contact details, trade categories, branch locations and compliance documents, accessible to everyone in the business
- Performance tracking — every order placed with a supplier builds a performance record covering delivery accuracy, lead times and dispute history, giving procurement teams the data to make better award decisions
- Document management and expiry alerts — insurance certificates, accreditations and trade memberships are stored against the supplier record and StoneRise sends alerts before they expire, so compliance is managed automatically rather than reactively
- Supplier branching — suppliers with multiple branches or depots are managed under a single profile, so the business has a complete picture of its relationship with each company regardless of which location fulfilled the order
