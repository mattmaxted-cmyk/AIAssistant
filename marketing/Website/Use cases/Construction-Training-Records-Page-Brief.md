### 2. Problem Block — 3 Pain Points
Present as short, punchy statements. Each should feel like something the reader recognises from their own day:

- CSCS cards, IPAF licences, asbestos awareness, first aid and dozens of other certifications are tracked across multiple spreadsheets with no central view. When a card expires, nobody is automatically told until someone checks
- Sending a worker to site with a lapsed certification is a compliance failure. It puts the business at risk, invalidates insurance in some circumstances and can result in enforcement action. A spreadsheet is not a reliable way to prevent it
- When a client or principal contractor asks for a training matrix, pulling one together takes hours. The information exists in different places, maintained by different people, and is never fully up to date at the same time

### 3. Solution Block — How StoneRise HR Works
Four features, each as a heading plus one sentence of benefit:

- Complete training records matrix — every employee's certifications are held in a single view covering internal training, external courses, vocational qualifications, CSCS cards and professional memberships
- Automatic expiry alerts — StoneRise tracks every certification expiry date and sends alerts in advance, so renewals are arranged before a card lapses rather than after
- Document storage and verification — certificates are stored against the employee record with QR code verification, giving site managers and external auditors instant access to proof of qualification
- Training history — a full log of every course completed, certificate issued and renewal arranged is available for each employee, making compliance reporting straightforward rather than a manual exercise
