### 2. Problem Block — 3 Pain Points
Present as short, punchy statements. Each should feel like something the reader recognises from their own day:

- Subcontractor records are spread across spreadsheets, email threads and filing systems. Nobody knows which version is current or whether the compliance documents are still valid
- PQQ checks are done inconsistently depending on who is running the tender. Subcontractors that should not be on the approved list get awarded packages because nobody has a complete picture
- When insurance documents or accreditations expire mid-contract, commercial teams only find out when there is a problem on site. By then it is already a risk

### 3. Solution Block — How Certify Works
Four features, each as a heading plus one sentence of benefit:

- Central subcontractor register — every subcontractor is held in a single platform-wide profile with contact details, trade categories, performance history and compliance status visible in one place
- Structured PQQ workflow — subcontractors complete a standardised pre-qualification process before they can be awarded a package, with dual approval pathways for new and returning companies
- Document expiry alerts — Certify tracks insurance certificates, accreditations and compliance documents and sends alerts before they expire, so nothing lapses without the commercial team knowing
- Performance tracking — every subcontractor builds a record of performance across all packages, giving commercial teams the data they need to make better award decisions
