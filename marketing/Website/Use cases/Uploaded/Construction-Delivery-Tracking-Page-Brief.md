### 2. Problem Block — 3 Pain Points
Present as short, punchy statements. Each should feel like something the reader recognises from their own day:

- When deliveries are receipted on paper or by phone, short deliveries get signed off in the rush of a busy site morning. The discrepancy is only noticed when the invoice does not match, and by then the supplier has already moved on
- Without a live view of delivery status across all open orders, office-based procurement teams are constantly chasing site managers for updates. Work stops because the materials are not there and nobody knew they were late
- Disputed deliveries with no photographic evidence or digital receipt record are almost impossible to resolve. The supplier says the full quantity was delivered. The site manager says it was not. There is nothing to prove either way

### 3. Solution Block — How StoneRise Procurement Works
Four features, each as a heading plus one sentence of benefit:

- Real-time delivery status — every open order shows current delivery status so procurement teams can see what is in transit, what has been received and what is outstanding without calling site
- Quick receipt on mobile — site managers confirm deliveries directly from their phone, logging the actual quantity received against the order at the point of delivery rather than hours later
- Delivery photos — site teams can attach photos at the point of receipt, creating a timestamped visual record that supports any dispute with a supplier
- Short delivery alerts — when the quantity received does not match the quantity ordered, StoneRise flags the discrepancy immediately so the procurement team can act before the delivery vehicle has left site
