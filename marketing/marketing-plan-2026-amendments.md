## 6. Google Paid Search — Pay Apps & Certify Opportunity Analysis

**Objective:** Assess whether paid search around "pay apps" and Certify-adjacent keywords is commercially viable

**Context:** Current Google campaign (3 focus keywords, £70/day) targets broad construction software terms. These have proven expensive and slow to convert. The question is whether niche, intent-rich keywords around payment applications and Certify can deliver better economics.

---

### Keyword Opportunity Analysis

**Target keyword categories:**

| Keyword | Est. Monthly Searches (UK) | Est. CPC | Competition | Intent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| "payment application construction" | 300–500 | £2–4 | Low | High |
| "pay app construction software" | 100–300 | £2–3 | Very Low | High |
| "construction payment application software" | 100–200 | £2–4 | Very Low | High |
| "subcontractor payment application" | 200–400 | £2–4 | Low | High |
| "certify construction software" | <100 | £1–2 | Very Low | Very High (branded) |
| "construction payment certification" | 100–200 | £2–3 | Low | High |
| "pay less notice construction" | 100–200 | £1–3 | Very Low | High |
| "construction application for payment template" | 200–500 | £1–2 | Low | Medium–High |

> **Note:** These are estimates based on keyword research principles. Validate exact volumes and CPCs in Google Keyword Planner before committing budget.

---

### Financial Model

**Assumptions:**

| Metric | Value | Source / Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Avg CPC (pay apps cluster) | £2.50 | Lower competition than broad construction software terms |
| Daily budget | £20–30 | Test budget |
| Clicks/day | 8–12 | £25/day ÷ £2.50 CPC |
| Landing page CTR to demo request | 3–5% | B2B SaaS niche, high-intent traffic |
| Demo booking rate from leads | 40–60% | Niche, qualified visitors |
| Target CPL | £50–100 | Significantly better than current £276 CPL on broad terms |

**Monthly projections at £25/day (£775/month):**

| Metric | Conservative | Realistic | Optimistic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clicks/month | 200 | 280 | 360 |
| Leads (3–5% CVR) | 6 | 11 | 18 |
| Demos booked (50% of leads) | 3 | 6 | 9 |
| CPL | £129 | £70 | £43 |
| Cost per demo | £258 | £129 | £86 |

---

### Is it worth doing?

**Yes — with conditions.**

The economics are materially better than the current broad campaign for three reasons:

1. **Lower CPCs.** "Pay apps" and payment application keywords are niche — large software companies (Sage, Oracle, Procore) are not bidding on these terms aggressively because it's too small a niche for them. StoneRise can compete at £2–4 CPC vs £7–10 on broad construction software terms.

2. **Higher intent.** Someone searching "payment application construction software" is already aware they have the problem and are actively seeking a solution. Conversion rates will be higher than broad awareness terms.

3. **Direct product match.** The search term maps exactly to Certify. There is no mismatch between keyword intent and landing page offer — which is one reason the current campaign underperforms.

**Recommended approach:**
- Build a dedicated Certify landing page (if not already live) before launching
- Start at £20–25/day as a test budget — do not scale until CPL is validated
- Run for 30 days, then evaluate CPL vs demo booking rate
- Only scale if CPL comes in below £100 and demos convert to pipeline

**Timing:** Launch in line with Certify going live (May 2026). Running paid search before the product is available risks burning budget on leads that cannot convert.

**Do not run this campaign until:**
- [ ] Certify is live and taking demos
- [ ] A dedicated Certify landing page exists
- [ ] UTM tracking is confirmed working in GA4
- [ ] Keyword Planner has validated volumes and CPCs
