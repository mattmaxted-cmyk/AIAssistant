### Why Meta (Not Google)

- **CPC economics:** Meta CPC is £1.50–£2.50. Google CPC for high-intent construction software keywords is £9–£25. At the current budget level, Meta generates dramatically more volume per pound spent.
- Google Ads were paused after testing. The decision was driven by CPC, not CPL directly (though they are linked). At £9–£25 per click vs £1.50–£2.50, the maths didn't support Google at this stage.
- Meta has a larger addressable audience (not limited by search volume) and strong visual creative advantages.

### Current Meta Setup (as of March 2026)

- **Daily spend:** £170/day
- **Focus:** Procurement campaign only
- **Active creatives:** 4 creatives, all messaging app formats (WhatsApp, ChatGPT, iMessage). These have been the best performers and are the only ones currently running.

### Meta Performance — Key Metrics

| Metric | Role | Current Status |
|--------|------|---------------|
| CTR | How well the creative stops the scroll | Just below industry standard |
| Conv rate (link click → lead) | Landing page / offer performance | Significantly below industry average |

**Important context on conversion rate:**

Our conversion event is a **demo request** — a high-commitment action. Most SaaS competitors offer a free trial with no credit card, which is a near-zero-friction conversion. A lower conversion rate versus benchmarks is expected and structurally defensible for two reasons:

1. Only people genuinely interested in the platform will complete a demo request form. This produces better close rates downstream and a CPQL naturally closer to CPL than at most SaaS companies.
2. Meta is an interest-based platform. Audiences are less precisely targeted than high-intent platforms like Google Search, where users are actively searching for a solution. Lower audience intent = lower conversion rate. This is a channel characteristic, not a failure of execution.

## Part 5: Metrics & KPIs

### Core Metric: Cost Per Qualified Lead (CPQL)

**CPQL replaces CPL as the primary performance metric.**

CPL counts every lead regardless of quality — it is a volume metric, not an efficiency metric. CPQL measures only leads that have passed qualification, making it a far more accurate representation of marketing spend effectiveness.

**Reporting lag:** CPQL has a 1–2 week lag. Qualification takes time after a lead comes in. This is expected and acceptable.

**How CPQL connects to wider commercial metrics:**
- CPQL is the foundation of CAC. CAC includes all marketing costs (tools, content, headcount, paid spend), where CPQL captures paid-channel efficiency specifically.
- CAC feeds the LTV:CAC ratio. Once we have sufficient customer LTV data, CPQL and CAC become the primary levers for evaluating whether marketing spend is sustainable and scalable.

### KPI Framework

| Metric | Role | Reporting Frequency |
|--------|------|---------------------|
| CTR | Creative performance (how well the creative generates clicks) | Daily |
| Conv rate (link click → lead) | Landing page / offer performance | Weekly |
| CPL | Volume indicator | Weekly |
| **CPQL** | **Core efficiency metric** | Weekly (1–2 week lag) |
| CAC | Blended acquisition cost across all marketing costs | Monthly |
| LTV:CAC | Commercial sustainability | Quarterly |

> Impressions and raw click volumes are not primary metrics. They are contextual sanity checks — used to confirm traffic quality is normal and that CTR/conv rate are not artificially inflated by unusual traffic. Success is not measured by impressions or clicks alone.

### Actual CPL Data (Feb 2–26, 2026)

| Channel | Actual CPL |
|---------|-----------|
| Combined (all channels) | £190 |
| Meta | £145 |

> The period 28 Feb – 5 Mar is excluded. A form error during this period prevented leads from being recorded, making that data unreliable.

### Why the Original CPL Targets Were Unachievable

CPL = CPC ÷ conversion rate.

- £1.50 CPC is the low end of Meta pricing. Actual CPC runs £1.50–£2.50.
- At £1.50 CPC and a 2.5% conversion rate (good to average for a standard lead gen form), CPL is **£60** — already double the original £30 target.
- At £1.50 CPC, hitting £30 CPL would require a **5% conversion rate**. This is not achievable in our funnel for two structural reasons:
  1. Our conversion event is a demo request, not a free trial. Most SaaS competitors offer a free trial with no credit card — a near-zero-friction action. A demo request is a high-commitment conversion. Our conversion rate will structurally always be lower than the benchmarks those CPL targets were based on.
  2. Meta is an interest-based platform. Audiences are less precisely targeted than high-intent platforms like Google Search. Lower intent = lower conversion rate. This is a channel characteristic, not an execution failure.

The £30 CPL target required best-case CPC and an unrealistically high conversion rate simultaneously. Neither holds in practice.

### Monthly Review Checklist

**Marketing:**
- [ ] Meta CPQL trending — is the trend improving or worsening week-on-week?
- [ ] CTR within normal range (not inflated by bot traffic or unusual time-of-day patterns)

- [ ] Inbound demos delivered this month

**Sales:**

### HubSpot Setup

- All cold leads uploaded as **non-marketing contacts** with status "Cold".
- Custom "Cold" list view created in HubSpot for filtering.
- **Custom property:** "Is Cold Import" (Yes/No) — applied to all imported cold leads. This identifier persists regardless of list changes.
- All contacts imported with HubSpot ID included.

### Workflow & Automation

- **Webhook:** Connects Instantly.ai and HubSpot.
- **Trigger:** Lead replies to cold email → webhook fires.
- **HubSpot workflow on webhook:**
  1. Updates lead status.
  2. Adds email response as a note on the contact record.
  3. Sets a task for the contact owner to review.

### Parallel Calling

Sales team cold calls the same "Cold" list simultaneously with the email sequence. Goal: intro call bookings → qualification → move to main sales pipeline.

## Part 20: Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Meta CPL/CPQL Deteriorates

**Trigger:** CPQL increases materially for 2+ consecutive weeks with no creative refresh or audience change to explain it.

**Action:**
- Pause bottom-performing ad sets
- Test new creative formats (customer testimonials, pain point static)
- Review landing page conversion rate — is the issue creative or post-click?

### Risk 2: Demo Held Rate <50%

**Trigger:** More than 35% no-shows for 2+ weeks.

**Action:**
- Improve confirmation sequence (add SMS reminder)
- Reduce scheduling friction (more available slots)
- Add incentive: setup fee waived for demo attendees

### Risk 3: Procurement Ecosystem Uptake <0.3%

**Trigger:** Month 8 review shows <0.3% uptake (below 0.5% Year 1 target).

**Action:**
- Launch contractor referral incentive (£50/subbie)
- In-product banner for subbies: "Try Procurement Free"

### Risk 4: Behind Customer Target by Month 8

**Trigger:** <32 customers by end of Month 8.

**Action:**
- Increase M9–12 budget by £5,000 (from opex contingency or held LinkedIn/webinar budget)
- Accelerate Procurement cold outbound
- Review LinkedIn paid ads viability at that point
