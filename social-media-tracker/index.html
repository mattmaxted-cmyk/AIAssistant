👥 ICP — Who We Are Posting To

Michael Loizias

Construction industry professionals — project managers, site managers, QSs, contractors, subcontractors. Industry peers. People who have done the job. Posts resonate with people who recognise the problems from lived experience. Tone is direct, personal, occasionally blunt. Posts range from personal/health/adversity to industry critique to StoneRise product content.

Stelios Ioannou

Commercial and financial decision-makers in construction — Managing Directors, CEOs, Finance Directors, Financial Controllers, Commercial Directors, business owners. People who control budgets and ask "what is the ROI?" before approving anything. Tone is calm, analytical, financially grounded. Posts focus on business case, cost of inefficiency, and return on investment.

StoneRise Company Page

Broader construction industry. Product features, company news, thought leadership, lead generation content.

✍️ Writing Style Summary

Michael Loizias

Direct and unvarnished. Personal and grounded. Problem-first for product posts. Uses specific numbers, names, timeframes. Short paragraphs. No hyphens or dashes of any kind. Signature close: "Keep Moving Forward" where appropriate. Ends with: #BuiltByConstruction4Construction #StoneRise #ChallengingTheStatusQuo

Stelios Ioannou

Commercially and financially grounded. Calm, measured, authoritative. Leads with the financial problem. Uses real numbers — percentages, £ figures, payback periods. Short paragraphs. No hyphens or dashes of any kind. Ends with a soft CTA ("let's talk", "worth doing the maths"). No emoji.
